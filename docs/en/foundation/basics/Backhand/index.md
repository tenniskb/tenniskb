# Backhand — The Mirror, The Whip & The Slice

The backhand is the shot that separates recreational players from competitive ones. For most club players in their fifties, the backhand is where matches are won or lost — not because it's the most important stroke, but because it's the one that breaks down under pressure first.

This section is the complete backhand system. The main article covers the three families of backhand you need (the two-hander, the one-hander, the slice). The companion articles go deeper on each variation — the low ball slice, the return of serve slice, the quick-reference cheat sheets, and the deep-dive on the one-handed version for those who play it.

Read the main article first, slowly. Then pick the variation that matches your game. Most 50+ recreational players benefit from a two-handed drive for baseline play and a slice for the rest. If you have the time and the patience, the one-hander is the most elegant shot in tennis, but it has a longer learning curve.

The cheat sheets in this section are designed to print and laminate. The deep dives are designed to read on a phone between matches. Use both.

When the backhand breaks down — and it will, at some point — come back to this section. The fixes are usually simpler than you think.
