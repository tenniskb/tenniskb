# The Gravity Serve

The serve is the only shot you fully control. Everything else is a response. So the serve is also the easiest place to find free points — if you can build a serve you trust.

This section covers the modern serve from the ground up. The main article introduces the gravity serve — the principle of using the body's natural falling motion rather than muscle power to generate pace. The companion articles cover the continental grip, the toss, the contact point, and the specific body positions that produce a serve you can hit ten times in a row.

The gravity serve is the most important concept here. If you can internalize it — fall, don't push — the rest of the serve falls into place. The continental grip is the second most important concept. Without it, the rest doesn't work.

Practice the toss first. A reliable toss is the foundation of every reliable serve. Once the toss is automatic, add the leg drive. Once the leg drive is automatic, add the racket path. Build the serve one layer at a time, like a house.

When the serve breaks down — double faults in pressure, second serves that sit up — come back to the gravity serve article. The fix is almost always in the leg drive or the toss, not the arm.
