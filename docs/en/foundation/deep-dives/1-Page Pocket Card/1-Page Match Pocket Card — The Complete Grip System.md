  EXCEPTION:          Drop shot stays at 2-3/10  (soft at contact)

  THE 4 POINT TYPES + GRIP CHANGES
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Serving point   = 0 changes   (Continental stays for serve + volley + slice)
  Returning point = 1-3 changes (forehand return = Semi-Western)
  Net point       = 0-1 changes (Continental stays at net)
  Mixed point     = 2-4 changes (the most common)

  THE 8 GRIP TRANSITIONS
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Pre-serve              → Continental                1 sec
  Serve → Forehand       → Continental → Semi-W      1 sec (bevel walk 2)

  Backhand → Forehand    → Continental → Semi-W      1 sec (bevel walk 2)

  Forehand → F return    → Semi-W (no change)        0 sec

  Back to home base      → Any → Continental         0.5-1 sec

  THE 5-SECOND RESET (between points)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  0-2 sec  WALK to the line
  2-3 sec  LOOK at the V (11, 12, 1, or 2 o'clock?)
  3-4 sec  CHECK the bevel (2, 3, 4, or 5?)
  4-5 sec  WALK to the position (bevel walk if needed)

  THE 4 PICKUP METHODS
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  HAMMER PICKUP    Hold out → lay hand → slide down → close (Continental)
                   For Continental, the universal pickup
  SHAKE HANDS      Pretend to shake hands with the racket
                   For Eastern/Semi-Western, natural feel
  BEVEL WALK       Start at Continental (11), walk 1-2 bevels clockwise
                   For changing during a match, 1 second
  INDEX FINGER     Extend the index finger down the side
                   For quick check during a match

  "Phập" = clean slice (good slice)

  THE 4 PROTECTED JOINTS
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  ✓ WRIST    (no wrist snap, natural underspin from open face)
  ✓ ELBOW    (pronation, not wrist snap, no varus torque)
  ✓ SHOULDER (6-inch punch, not full swing)
  ✓ KNEE     (via 70% sprint on slice approach)

  4. Forget to change at net (fix: bevel walk before the net)

## HOW TO USE THIS CARD

### Before the Match

| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| **1** | Read the card on the way to the court |
| **2** | Pick the 3-5 cues that fit today's situation |
| **3** | Carry the card in the side pocket of your bag |

### During the Match

| Moment | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Before each serve** | Glance at the V (11 or 1 o'clock?) |
| **Between points** | Use the 5-second reset (walk-look-check-walk) |
| **At changeover** | Read 1 section of the card (15 sec) |
| **After a bad point** | Find the cue that fits (e.g., "V at 11 = Continental") |
| **After a good point** | Find the cue that fits (e.g., "Forearm rotation, not wrist") |

### After the Match

| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| **1** | Review: which cues worked? Which didn't? |
| **2** | Note: which errors did you make? (6 common errors) |
| **3** | Pick 1 cue to focus on next match |
| **4** | Carry the card again (or print a fresh one) |

### Print Specifications

| Spec | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Paper** | Card stock (110-150 lb), matte (less glare) |
| **Size** | 4.25" × 5.5" (fits side pocket) or 5" × 7" (more readable) |
| **Color** | Black text on white (most readable); red for "5 cues to remember" (eye-catching) |
| **Laminate** | Yes (matte laminate, 3-5 mil thickness) |
| **Fold** | Bi-fold (8.5" × 11" → 5.5" × 8.5") or tri-fold (4.25" × 11" → 4.25" × 3.7") |
| **Pocket** | Side pocket of tennis bag (NOT the racket compartment) |
