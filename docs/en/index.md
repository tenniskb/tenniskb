# Welcome to the Tennis Knowledge Base

This is the home of the Tennis Knowledge Base — a growing library of practical, honest writing about how to play tennis, why the techniques work, and what it becomes when you take the game seriously.

## What You'll Find Here

The Knowledge Base is organized in three levels, following the way tennis is actually learned:

- **[Foundation](./foundation/index.md)** — where every journey begins. Grip, stance, footwork, the basic strokes, returning serve, doubles patterns, mental game.
- **[Advanced](./advanced/index.md)** — refining what you know. Biomechanics, embodied cognition, fascia, proprioception, reflex arcs, two-engine coordination, the X-factor.
- **[Elite](./elite/index.md)** — for those with years of practice. The complete elite manual, the questions that only surface after long work.

Each section has its own Basics and Deep Dives. Basics are the practical work. Deep Dives are the articles that go further on a single topic.

## How This Knowledge Base Is Different

I get asked often: *"How is this different from other tennis resources?"*

A few honest answers:

**It's written for the 3.5 to 4.5 player who wants real understanding, not quick fixes.** The technical terms are there because they're useful. The mechanical explanations are there because they actually explain. If something can be said in plain English, it's said in plain English.

**It assumes you want to play, not just read.** Every article includes practices you can take to the court. The articles are maps; the territory is your own body and your own ball-striking.

**It treats biomechanics seriously.** Tennis is a body-mechanics problem as much as it is a stroke problem. The deeper work — ground reaction forces, kinetic chain, unit turn, the late acceleration L-angle — is what separates a 3.5 from a 4.5.

**It's built for the 50+ player too.** Many strokes have a slice variation specifically for the older body. The slice backhand, the slice return, the slice approach — these aren't compromise shots. They're often the highest-percentage choice at any age.

**It evolves.** As I learn more, the articles get better. The latest versions are always here.
