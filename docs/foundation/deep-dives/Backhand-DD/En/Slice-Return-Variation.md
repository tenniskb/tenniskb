# The Return of Serve Slice: Safety First

A big serve at 3.5 level usually wins through pace and spin that throws off your rhythm, not raw power alone. Swing back hard with topspin against it and you'll mostly find errors. The slice return is your redirection tool — and past 50, you don't need to be a hero here. A deep, slow-paced slice landing in the middle of the service box beats a winner attempt into the net ten times out of ten.

The technique is "block and redirect," and it starts with something non-negotiable: the split-step, timed to your opponent's contact. Skip it and you're already late. From there, don't swing — the server has already given you all the pace you need, so your job is simply to block the ball, keeping the racket short, compact, and steady. With your Continental grip already in hand, just angle the racket face as the ball arrives to direct it deep into your opponent's court. Think of it as a volley on the return.

Where you send it matters. Deep and down the middle removes your opponent's angle entirely and is the safest target on the return. At the server's feet works well specifically against a serve-and-volley player following their serve in. And wide to the backhand opens up the court for whatever you want to do with your next shot.

**A drill worth building.** Stand 6 meters from a wall, throw the ball hard against it, and slice-block it back as it rebounds toward you. Focus on quiet hands and a firm wrist that absorbs the pace rather than fighting it. Ten returns landing deep — near the baseline on the wall — is your goal.
