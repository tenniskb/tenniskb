# Cú Giao Bóng Bằng Slice

The slice approach is the senior-friendly way to the net. For recreational players in their fifties, charging the net behind a topspin approach is a recipe for passing shots. The slice stays low, buys you time, and forces the passer to hit up.

This section covers the slice approach shot — the technique, when to use it, and how to transition from baseline to net without getting passed. The main article is the full coaching guide. The companion article is the Vietnamese-language version for those who prefer to read in their first language.

The key idea is depth. A deep slice approach is unhittable for most passers. A short slice approach is a gift. Learn to slice with depth first, then add the transition.

Practice the slice approach in two phases. Phase one: hit ten slice approach shots from the baseline, all landing past the service line. Phase two: hit ten slice approach shots followed by a transition to the net and a volley. The transition is the hard part. The shot is the easy part.

When the approach doesn't work — usually because the slice is floating instead of skidding — come back to the technique article. The fix is in the contact point and the racket path, not the pace.
