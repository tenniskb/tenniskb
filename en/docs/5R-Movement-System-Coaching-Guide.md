# The 5R Movement System

I get asked this a lot: "Why do I feel rushed on court even when the ball isn't hit that hard?" Almost every time, the answer isn't the opponent's pace — it's a broken link somewhere in your movement loop.

Watch any good player between shots and you'll notice they're never really "running to the ball." They're cycling through five connected phases, over and over, point after point: Ready, Read, React, Respond, Recover. I call it the 5R loop. Miss one link and the whole chain buckles — you end up defending a ball that a cleaner mover would've handled easily.

## The Five Phases

**Ready.** Before your opponent even makes contact, you need an athletic base — knees bent, weight forward, racket up. This is the foundation everything else builds on.

**Read.** As the ball leaves their strings, you're tracking it — trajectory, spin, pace — with your eyes, not guessing. The earlier you read it, the more time you buy yourself for everything downstream.

**React.** This is your split step, timed to land right as they make contact. It preloads your legs like a spring, ready to fire in any direction.

**Respond.** Now you actually move — explosive steps, a good crossover if it's wide — getting you to the ball with time to spare and a stroke you can actually execute, not just survive.

**Recover.** The one everyone forgets. The instant you've hit your shot, you're already moving back toward your ideal court position for the next ball. This isn't optional recovery time — it's the start of the next cycle.

## Where It Breaks Down

The two failure modes I see constantly:

| What happens | Why it happens | What it costs you |
|---|---|---|
| Ball watching | You admire your shot instead of recovering | Caught flat-footed for the next one |
| Late reaction | No split step, no early read | You're always a half-step behind, always defending |

Both of these come from treating the loop as five separate things instead of one continuous motion. It isn't. Ready flows into Read flows into React — there's no seam where one stops and the next begins.

## Training It

Here's the thing — you can't drill "recovery" in isolation and expect it to show up in a rally. Try this: have your coach or partner feed you a ball, hit your shot, and force yourself to execute a specific recovery step before the next ball comes. Do this over and over until your nervous system stops treating the stroke and the recovery as two separate events. They're one event. That's the whole point of the 5R system — you're not learning five skills, you're wiring one loop.
