# Body Volley Protocol

There's one volley where everything you've learned about body weight and forward step has to get thrown out entirely: the ball hit directly at your body. There's no time or space to step aside and set up your normal power triangle from the side, no forward step available to transfer weight into. Your "stick" here has to come from somewhere else completely — isometric grip rigidity, and a follow-through so short it barely qualifies as one.

## The Problem With Trying to Swing Anyway

Attempt a standard volley swing on a body shot and you'll get one of two outcomes. Either you get framed — your racket collides with your own ribs or elbow as you try to swing, producing a genuine mis-hit. Or you get what I'd call a net dump — a scooping motion trying to get under a body-height ball and clear the net, which produces a soft, short ball that just sits up for your opponent.

## The Actual Fix: Isometric Rigidity

Since your body weight can't contribute here, the entire structural support for the shot has to come from your grip. That means a real spike — grip pressure surging to nearly 9 out of 10, the highest point on the whole scale, right at the moment of contact. That maximum rigidity in your hand creates the firm surface that redirects the ball with real depth, substituting for the body-weight contribution you don't have access to. The physics are straightforward: eliminate the body's contribution, and grip-generated resistance is the only thing left to build a wall with.

## Your Follow-Through Is Almost Nothing

The finish on a body volley needs to stay under 3 inches — a pure staccato hit. Try to swing any further and you risk hitting your own ribs or elbow, you introduce rotational force that destabilizes your racket face angle, and you're asking for an arm path that the tight body-shot contact zone simply can't accommodate.

This is a deliberate departure from your normal step-hit-step rhythm. That cadence requires forward movement, and on a body shot, there is no forward movement available to you. The body volley is pure stillness — rigidity, contact, immediate release, nothing else.

## Standard Volley vs. Body Volley

| Feature | Standard Volley | Body Volley |
|---|---|---|
| Body weight contribution | Primary power source | Unavailable |
| Grip pressure at contact | Around 8/10 | Around 9/10 |
| Follow-through | Step continues through the ball | Under 3 inches — staccato |
| Power triangle | Full structure maintained | Compressed, arm close to body |
| Recovery | Flows through, trailing foot continues | Immediate rebound, no follow-through to recover from |

## Why Opponents Target This Specifically

Body volleys aren't random — they're deliberately aimed at you by opponents who understand that a body shot eliminates your power triangle and forces this wrist-less, staccato response out of you. This is exactly why it's worth practicing on purpose: it's one of the most commonly exploited vulnerabilities at net against anyone who's figured out how it works.
